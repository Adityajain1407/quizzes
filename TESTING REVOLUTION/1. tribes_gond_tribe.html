THE GOND TRIBE

Comprehensive Study Notes — Traditions, Culture, Religion, Language & Economy

1. OVERVIEW & DEMOGRAPHICS

1.1 Population & Rank

  • Largest tribe in India
  • 2nd largest tribe in Madhya Pradesh (MP)
  • Total population: 50.93 lakh (33.25% of the tribal population in MP)
  • Self-designation / Original name: Koitor

1.2 Geographic Distribution

  • Primarily found in South-East (Dakshin-Poorv) Madhya Pradesh
  • Concentrated districts: Chhindwara, Mandla

1.3 Sub-Tribes

  • Major sub-tribes: Agaria, Pardhan, Ojha, Solhas.

1.4 Gotras (Clans)

  • System known as "Kur"
  • Major gotras: Rajgond, Daurgond, Muriagond (extream backward class) economically

1.5 Village Administrative Structure

  • Village head is called: Majhi (Mukhiya)
  • Village officials include: Muqaddam, Kotwar, Likhari

1.6 Food & Sacred Drinks

  • Traditional beverages: Pej, Mahua, devanna

1.7 Death Rituals

  • Post-death ritual: Dashmani (ceremony performed after death)

3. FOLK SONGS (LOK GEET)

3.1 Marriage Song Timeline (Vivah Geet Chakra)

Songs are performed at specific stages of a wedding ceremony:

Stage Song Name Hindi Theme
Groom gets ready Nahadora (Nahodora) नाहोडोरा Groom's preparation
Procession on the way (Barat) Dadariya दद्रिया Boasting / Heroic (Veer Ras / Garv)
Wedding Banter Saila Bhadauni सैला भदौनी Teasing / Abuses / Maza aur Gaaliyaan
In-Laws Meet (Samdhi-Samdhin Meelan) Sajni सजनी Meeting of in-laws
Procession Returns (Barat Wapsi) Kahrawa कहवा Return celebration

3.2 Thematic Folk Songs

  • Devotional Songs (Shraddha)
    • Jas Geet (जस गीत): Praise of Budhadev (Budhadev ki Stuti)
  • Romantic Songs (Prem)
    • Jhooma Jhoomar (झूमा झूमर): Sung in memory of a lost partner (Sathi ki yaad mein)
    • Setam (सेतम): Satirizing / teasing the beloved (Priya par vyangya)
  • Seasonal Songs (Mausami)
    • Phaag (फाग): Sung during the month of Phalguna (Phalgun maas mein)
  • Work / Dance Songs (Karma / Nritya)
    • Karma (कर्मा): Has 4 types — Thadha, Lahki, Langda, Ragini
  • Epic Songs (Gatha)
    • Gondwani (गोंडवानी): Tales of Gond warriors (Gond Yoddhaaon ki gathayen)

4. FOLK THEATER (LOK NATYA)

4.1 Main Forms

  • Swang (स्वांग)
  • Natya (नाट्य)
  • Gammat (गम्मत)

4.2 Regional Spotlight — Shahdol

  • Shahdol is famous for the Swang theater form
  • Swang is sung on the "Barhar" day of the marriage ceremony (Vivah ke "Barhar" din Swang)

4.3 Legendary Artist

  • Mithubaks of Mudki Village: Renowned as a highly skilled Swang dancer (Mudi village ke Mithubaks — kushāl Swang nartaak)

6. EPIC STORY TRADITIONS

6.1 Comparison: Mainstream Epics vs. Gond Tradition

Epic Mainstream Version Gond Tradition Key Difference
Pandavani (पांडवानी) Mainstream: Bhandavani (Mahabharata) Bhima > Arjun (Bheem is hero, not Arjun) Values physical strength over archery
Ramayani (रामायणी) Mainstream: Pyilvomdwar (Ramayana) Lakshman is the hero (Lakshman Nayak Hain) Lakshman had 7 marriages; known as "Gond Lamsnai" (service marriage)

7. GOND FESTIVALS (PARV / TYOHAR)

7.1 Complete Festival Quick-Reference Table

Festival Timing Key Focus & Ritual

Navkhani Bhadrapaksha 9th (9th day after Ganesh Chaturthi) Kuldevi worship with new rice; celebrated when paddy ears emerge; Ancestor worship

Bakbandhi (बकबंधी) Ashadh Purnima (Guru Purnima) Nature's Rakshabandhan; Baiga extracts Palash roots, colours with turmeric, ties fibers to youth's wrists; Legend: Lingadev tied to Naga Baiga after fire test
Hardhili (हरधिली) Shravas Amavasya (New moon of Shravana) Marks completion of Kharif sowing; Bulls worshipped and loosened from ploughs

7.2 Festival Details

  • Madai (मड़ई) — Harvest & Community Festival
    • Concept: Harvest happiness and Village deity worship
    • Rituals: Meeting loved ones; buying/selling essential items
    • Exam Fact: Famous in Mandla & Dindori districts; Dhanwahi village Mela Haat is highly famous
  • Navkhani (नवखानी) — New Rice Festival
    • Concept: Celebrated when paddy ears emerge; Kuldevi worship with new rice
    • Timing: 9th day of Bhadrapaksha (after Ganesh Chaturthi)
  • Bidri (बिदरी) — Cloud Worship Festival
    • Timing: Jyeshtha month
    • Ritual: Water offered to Thakur Dev before sunrise; white chicken sacrifice; grains distributed to mix with seeds
    • Meaning: From "Badri" or "Badal" — worshipping earth and nature
  • Bakbandhi (बकबंधी) — Nature's Rakshabandhan
    • Timing: Full moon of Ashadh (Guru Purnima)
    • Ritual: Baiga extracts Palash roots, colours with turmeric, ties fibers to youth's wrists
    • Legend: Lingadev tied it to Naga Baiga after a fire test
  • Hardhili (हरधिली) — Kharif Completion
    • Timing: New moon of Shravas (Shravan) month
    • Concept: Marks completion of Kharif sowing; Bulls worshipped and loosened from ploughs
  • Cherta (छेरता) — Children's Festival
    • Timing: Full moon of Paush
    • Ritual: Children go door-to-door with bamboo sticks and bags
    • Chant: "Cher Chitta Cherta"
  • Larukaj (लारूकाज) — Prosperity & Animal Sacrifice Ritual
    • Deity: Narayan Dev
    • Ritual Significance: Symbolizes the marriage of the pig; involves pig sacrifice
    • Purpose: Believed to bring happiness and prosperity to the family
  • Meghnath (मेघनाथ) — Supreme Deity Festival
    • Timing: First fortnight of Phalguna
    • Focus: Worship of the supreme deity; ancestor worship
    • Alternative name: Khandera / Khatta.

8. RELIGION & DEITIES (DEVTA AUR DHARM)

8.1 Foundations of Faith

  • Supreme Deity: Meghnath (मेघनाथ) — Highest deity of the Gonds; celebrated in first fortnight of Phalguna
    • Alternative names: Khandera or Khatta (खंडेरा या खट्टा)
  • Lingadev & Koyapunem: The origin of the Gond religious system is traced to Lingadev; their worship system is called Koyapunem
  • Totemism (टोटेमवाद): The Totem holds a highly significant place in religious beliefs

8.2 Priesthood System (Exam Alert)

  • Traditional Priests: Often belong to the Baiga tribe
  • Specific Gond Priest: Officially referred to as "Dewari" (देवरी)
  • Core General Beliefs: Earth, water, and air are ruled by gods; strong belief in ancient spirit worship
  • Modern Note: Most of the Gond population now follows Hinduism

8.3 Primary Tribal Deities

  • Bada Dev (बड़ा देव) (Shivji)
    • The Almighty God (Sarvashaktimaan Ishwar)
    • Forms and other names: Baraka Dev, Baradeva, Lingo, Rai Lingo, Barapen
  • Dulha Dev (दूल्हा देव)
    • The most important deity of the tribe (Janjaati ke sabse mahatvapurna devta)
    • Form of Lord Shankar
    • Rides a white horse
    • Located towards the East in the village
  • Thakur Dev (ठाकुर देव)
    • Village protector deity
    • Situated under a tree outside the village (Gaon ke bahar ped ke neeche)
  • Narayan Dev (नारायण देव)
    • Placed at the threshold of the house to prevent obstacles (Badhaaon ko rokne ke liye ghar ki dahleej par)
  • Ghamsen Dev (घमसेन देव)
    • Placed near the elder god (Bada Dev)
    • Marked by a buried pillar (Gade hue khambhe ke roop mein)
  • Khairmai Devi (खैरमाई देवी)
    • Village protector goddess
    • Situated under the Mahua tree (Mahua ped ke neeche)
  • Banjarin Mai (बंजारिन माई)
    • Forest goddess (Vandevi)
    • Also known as: Ratri Mai, Andheri Mai
  • Khair Khunt Muthiya Dev (खैर खूंट मुठिया देव)
    • Shepherd God and Cow Protector (Charwaha devta aur gaay rakshak)
  • Nageshwar Dev (नागेश्वर देव)
    • Represents Snake Worship (Naag Puja ka Prateek)
  • Sharda Mai (शारदा माई)
    • The goddess who bestows blessings (Aashirvad dene wali devi)
  • Shitala Mai (शीतला माई)
    • A loving goddess
    • Considered the younger sister of Sharda Mai

9. GOND LANGUAGE (BHASHA)

9.1 Etymology — Origin of the Word "Gond"

  • Derived from the Telugu word "Gonda" or "Gond"
  • Meaning: Mountain (Parvat / Pahad)
  • Result: Mountain Dwellers = Gonds (Pahaadon mein rehne wale = Gond)

9.2 Language Family

  • Root: Dravidian Language Family (Dravid Bhasha Parivar)
  • Primary language: Gondi (गोंडी)
  • Other dialects: Halbi, Bhatri, Dorli
  • Gondi is classified as a "middle-class" language — originally an unwritten dialect

9.3 Official State Recognition

  • Gondi was officially included in the Madhya Pradesh Pathya Pustak Nigam (Textbook Corporation) in 2019

9.4 Regional Dialect Variation

  • Pure Gondi (Shuddh Gondi) — Districts:
    • Chhindwara (छिंदवाड़ा)
    • Pandhurna (पांढुर्णा)
    • Seoni (सिवनी)
    • Betul (बैतूल)
    • Narmadapuram (नर्मदापुरम)
    • East Nimar / Khandwa (पूर्वी निमाड़ / खंडवा)
    • Balaghat (बालाघाट)
  • Distorted Gondi (Vikrit Gondi) — Districts:
    • Mandla (मंडला)
    • Dindori (डिंडोरी)
    • Shahdol (शहडोल)
    • Rewa (रीवा)

Note: Distorted regions show heavy Chhattisgarhi language influence

10. GOND ECONOMIC LIFE

10.1 Main Occupation

  • Primary occupation: Agriculture (Krishi)
  • Main agricultural tool: Hal (The Plow)
  • Traditional Gond term for agriculture: "Bari" (बाड़ी)

10.2 Alternative Livelihoods

  • Animal husbandry (Pashupalan)
  • Collection of forest produce (Vanopaj Sangrah)

10.3 Special Agricultural Terms

  • Harwahi (हरवाही): Farming engaged in by those without bulls or land (Bina bail ya zameen waalon dwara ki jaane wali kheti)

10.4 Crop Seasons(imp***)

Gond Name Standard Name Season Crops
Siari (सियारी) Kharif / Monsoon Rainy season Paddy (Dhan), Kodon Kutki (millets), Oilseeds (Tilhan), Ramtila, Rice (Chawal)
Unhari / Unyari (उन्हाड़ी) Rabi / Winter Winter season Wheat (Gehun), Gram (Chana), Mustard (Sarson), Lentils (Masoor), Linseed (Alsi)

11. SOCIAL INSTITUTIONS — THE GHOTUL

11.1 Ghotul (घोटुल)

  • The traditional youth home / dormitory of the Gond tribe (Gond janjaati ka paramparik yuva griha)
  • A unique socio-educational institution for young men and women
  • Key literary source: "The Muria and their Ghotul" — by anthropologist Verrier Elwin (1947)
  • This is a highly significant ethnographic publication for tribal studies

11.2 Dhanda (धांदा)

  • Traditional riddles and puzzles used to test intelligence and pass time
  • Samay bitaane aur buddhi parikshan ke liye paramparik paheliyan
