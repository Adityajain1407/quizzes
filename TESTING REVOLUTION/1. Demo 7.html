THE BAIGA TRIBE

Sons of the Forest
Comprehensive Study Notes for M.P. PSC / UPSC

1. MACRO CLASSIFICATION & REGIONAL IDENTITY

1.1 Racial & Community Classification

  • Race: Dravidian Community
  • Sub-race: Proto-Australoid Race
  • Tribal affiliation: Sub-species / branch of the Gond tribe
  • The Baiga are considered a distinct sub-tribe that evolved from the Gond community

1.2 Primary Region(imp)***

  • Most important tribe in the Southern / Eastern Madhya Pradesh region
  • Concentrated in the Eastern Satpura range, between the Satpura and Maikal mountain ranges
  • Key districts: Mandla, Dindori, Balaghat, Sidhi
  • The primary Baiga settlement area within Dindori district is called Baigachak
  • Their entire geographical zone is heavily covered with Sal (Saj) trees

2. IDENTITY MATRIX

2.1 Official Status

  • Declared a Backward / Primitive Tribe by the Government of India

2.2 Self-Identity & Alternative Names(imp)***

  • King of the Forest / Son of Nature (Jungle ke Raja / Prakriti ke Putra)
  • Dharti Putra (Son of the Earth)
  • Bichhawar
  • Aadiswami / Aboriginals (as described by Verrier Elwin)

2.3 Roles & Titles within the Community

  • Priest (Purohit / Pujari): serves as village priest and doctor
  • Panda: sub-role under the Priest
  • Bewat: sub-role under the Priest
  • The priest figure (Panda/Bewat) serves dual roles - both Village Priest and Doctor

3. CULTURE, BELIEFS & LITERATURE

3.1 Supreme Deity

  • Name: Thakur Dev (ठाकुर देव)
  • Role: Creator of the Universe according to Baiga tradition

3.2 Key Literature(imp)***

  • Book Title: 'The Baiga'
  • Author: Verrier Elwin (Verrier Elwin / वेरियर एल्विन)
  • Other descriptions of Baigas in the book: Magicians or Physicians (Jadoogar ya Chikitsak)
  • Also referred to as Aadiswami / Aboriginals
  • Note: Another famous work by Verrier Elwin is 'Muria and Their Ghotul'

4. ORIGIN MYTH (According to Russell & Hiralal)

4.1 Creation Lineage Flowchart

  • God (Ishwar) created Nag Baiga (नाग बैगा) & Nagi Bagin (नागी बाघिन)
  • First child of Nag Baiga & Nagi Bagin = Baiga
  • Second child = Gond

4.2 Human Race Origin

  • First couple from creation = Baigas (पहले जोड़े से बैगा)
  • Second couple from creation = Gonds (दूसरे जोड़े से गोंड)

4.3 Cliffhanger Lore

  • Naga Baiga sent Crow Kunwar (Kag Kunwar / काग कुंवर) to find the earth (dharti)
  • The crow is given special respect / sacred status by the Baiga people

5. DEMOGRAPHICS & HISTORY

5.1 Census & Historical Classification

  • Census 1931: Officially recorded as 'Animists' (Jeevvadi)
  • Census 1941: Classified as Hindus
  • Russell & Hiralal classified them as tribes from Bengal & Bihar

5.2 Population Statistics (2011 Census)

  • Total Population : 4.15 Lakh (approx.)
  • Percentage of total ST (Scheduled Tribe) population: 2.71%
  • Highest population district: Mandla District (मंडला जिला)

5.3 Heritage & Sacred Beliefs (imp)***

  • Naga Baiga is known as 'Bhumik' (Creator of the Earth)
  • The Baiga believe the Earth is their daughter; hence they refuse to plow the land
  • Special reverence / sacred respect given to crows
  • Honorific words within the community: Dewar and Devarin (देवार और देवारिन)

6. GEOGRAPHICAL DISTRIBUTION

6.1 Key Regions

  • Eastern Satpura (Purvi Satpura): Dense settlement between the Satpura and Maikal mountain ranges
  • Baigachak : Primary settlement zone located in Dindori district
  • Sal Forests (Sal Van): The entire geographic area is heavily covered with Sal trees

6.2 Key Districts (imp)***

  • Dindori
  • Mandla
  • Balaghat
  • Sidhi

7. PHYSICAL CHARACTERISTICS

  • Height: Medium (Madhyam kad)
  • Hair: Straight (Seedhe baal)
  • Nose: Flat (Chapti naak)
  • Complexion: Dark / Black (Kaala rang)
  • Body: Well-built (Sugathit sharir)

8. SUB-TRIBES (According to Russell)

8.1 Bharotiya — Highest Position

  • Meaning of name: 'Sustainer' (Poshan karne wala)
  • Are in their original / most primitive state
  • Do NOT use ploughs
  • Distinctive feature: Keep long hair

8.2 Binjhwar — Most Civilized

  • Are the most civilized sub-group
  • Big landlords
  • Call themselves 'Vindhyanchal' or 'Vindhwaar'

8.3 Kathamaina / Kodavan

  • Woodworkers (Lakdi ka kaam karne wale)

8.4 Mundipa

  • Those who keep a clean moustache (Saaf moonch rakhne wale)

8.5 Gondmaina — Lowest Position

  • Lowest position in the tribe's hierarchy

8.6 Other Sub-tribes

  • Narotiya (नरोतिया)
  • Ramena (रमैना)
  • Nahar (नाहर)

9. CLAN (GOTRA) & GARH SYSTEM

The Baiga are organized as a branch of Baiga Bhumiya.

9.1 Major Gotras (Clans)

  • Maravi
  • Dhruve
  • Markam
  • Parteti
  • Tekam

9.2 Classification Basis

  • Jaat Karma: Hada-Rathudiya (work / occupation-based)
  • Qualities (Gun): Nidhuniya
  • Residence: Ghangharia

9.3 Strongholds / Major Garhs

  • Deori (देवरी): Rice-eating hunter
  • Sailatala (सैलातला): Sarai tree
  • Koshiyari Khusru (कोशियारी खुसरो): Owl bird
  • Ghata (घाटा), Tantar (टंटार), Bangar (बांगर), Bhusando (भूसंडो), Kakaria (ककरिया), Chada (चाड़ा), Talia (तलिया)

10. SOCIAL STRUCTURE & FAMILY SYSTEM

10.1 Core Family Features

  • Patriarchal Society : Baiga society is predominantly male-dominated
  • Nuclear Families : Baigas mostly prefer to live in nuclear family units
  • Separate Residence : When a son marries, he builds another house nearby (separate from the father's)
  • Respect for Elders : Parents willingly live with any son; younger ones always obey their elders

10.2 Core Values Regarding Women

  • Men make the rules of society
  • However, women hold HIGH social standing
  • Disrespecting a woman = Disrespecting the entire Baiga community

11. THE MANDI: BAIGA PANCHAYAT SYSTEM

The internal social organization of the Baiga is highly structured. The meeting/assembly of the Panchas is called the Mandi.

11.1 Governance Hierarchy(imp)***

  • Muqaddam (मुकद्दम) — Village Head (Hereditary): Chief manager; receives marriage baraats; handles all government functions
  • Diwan (दीवान) — Assistant to Muqaddam (Lineage-based): Convenes the Panchayat; invites people to marriages; entertains guests
  • Dewar (देवार) — Priest / Panda (Bhumiya): Performs all religious worship and Bidri puja
  • Samrath (समरथ): Arranges rations for social functions, guests, and government officers
  • Kotwar (कोटवार) — Government Servant (any caste): Responsible for village security; announces Muqaddam's decisions; reports births and deaths

12. VILLAGE ARCHITECTURE

Villages are located in upper mountains and forest banks (Paharon ke upari hisson aur janglon ke kinare)

12.1 Key Architectural Terms(imp)***

  • Tola or Purva (टोला या पुरवा): The circular-shaped village settlement pattern
  • Mero (मेरो): A protective wall made of vertical sticks surrounding the village/house
  • Bangla (बंगला): The main house used for living and meetings; includes a front shed for animals and a chicken coop

13. TRADITIONAL ATTIRE

13.1 Men's Clothing

  • Garments: Dhoti (धोती), Shirt (शर्ट), Bandi (बंडी), Turban (पगड़ी)
  • Special cloth: Patka or Langoti (पटका या लंगोटी)
  • Accessories: Bracelets (Kangan) and Earrings (Jhumke)
  • Grooming: Big hair like women, but with less beard and moustache

13.2 Women's Clothing

  • Main garment: Kapchi (कपची) — a saree wrapped from the waist to above the knees
  • Traditional Lugdas (3 types): Mungi (मुंगी), Bigra (बिगरा), Chagadaria (छगदरिया)

13.3 Women's Ornaments

Body Part Ornament Name
Hair (Baal) Kandri (कंदरी)
Neck (Gardan) Sutiya (सुतिया) & Havel (हवेली)
Arms (Bhujayein) Haraiya (हरैया)
Waist (Kamar) Waistband (कमरबंद)
Feet (Pair) Anklets (Payal) & Todal (टोडल)

Tarakul Jewellery (तरकुल आभूषण): A mandatory sign of marriage for married Baiga women (Suhaag ka anivarya prateek)

14. CULINARY TRADITIONS (KHAN-PAAN)

14.1 Diet Overview

  • Diet: Both vegetarian and non-vegetarian
  • Favorite food: Fish (Machli)
  • Observation (Dom Moraes): There is a lack of carbohydrates in their daily diet
  • Meals are traditionally eaten on Mahlon leaves, which serve as both plate and bowl

14.2 Daily Diet Timeline(imp)***

Meal Name Timing
Morning Baasi (बासी) Eaten in the morning
Afternoon Pej Eaten in the afternoon
Night Biyari Eaten at night

14.3 Pej (पेज) — The Main Beverage/Food

  • Pej is the main food/beverage of the Baiga people
  • Prepared from: Kodo-Kutki grains and Maize (Kodo-Kutki aur Makka se nirmit)

15. SACRED BIRTH RITUALS (PAVITR JANM SANSKAR)

15.1 Birth Ritual Steps in Order

  • Step 1 — Sunamai (सुनामाई): The elder lady of the village/house who assists in the birth (acts as midwife)
  • Step 2 — Jhalar (झालर): The child's father cuts the hair on the child's head
  • Step 3 — Bardina (बरदीना): All types of paddy are kept together at the time of birth
  • Step 4 — Mand Celebration (मांड का जश्न): The house owner orders 1-2 casks of 'Mand' (pure liquor made from Mahua) to celebrate the birth

16. MARRIAGE RULES & CUSTOMS

16.1 Marriage Rules

  • Same caste / same sub-tribe marriage: PROHIBITED (Nishedh)
  • Same Gotra / Clan marriage: ALLOWED (Sweekriit)
  • Polygamy (Bahupani Pratha): Multiple wives are permitted

16.2 The Groom's Ride Tradition

  • Grooms traditionally prefer riding an elephant (haathi) during the marriage procession
  • If a real elephant is unavailable, an artificial elephant is made from a charpai (Charpai se naqli haathi banaya jaata hai)

16.3 Sacred Marriage Symbol(crow ko mante hai)

  • Karas Kaua (करस कौआ): A sacred symbol/deity specially established during the Baiga marriage ceremony

16.4 Six Types of Baiga Marriages

  1. Mangni / Chad (मंगनी या चढ़ विवाह): The boy's father visits the girl's courtyard and asks for acceptance through symbols. Features Bilma & Dadriya songs and Karma & Pardhani dances. Special ritual: Matikhnauni (माटिखनौनी) — tradition of Mangarmati on Tuesday morning
  2. Uthwa (उठवा विवाह): The bride's side participates but the groom's side bears the entire expense
  3. Chor (चोर विवाह): Elopement. The boy and girl run away with mutual consent
  4. Paithul (पैठुल विवाह): An unmarried girl enters the boy's house of her own free will
  5. Lamsena (लमसेना विवाह): Service marriage. If the boy's family cannot afford the marriage expenses, the boy lives at and serves the girl's father's home. The groom is called 'Lamsena' or 'son-in-law of the house'
  6. Udhriya (उधरिया विवाह): Marriage facilitated by a known relative without the approval of parents from both sides

17. DEATH RITUALS & FINAL RITES

17.1 General Death

  • Tradition permits both burying (dafnaana) and burning (jalaana)
  • Final death rites are decided in the Mandi (meeting of the Panchas)

17.2 If a Sick Person Dies

  • The body is strictly buried (Dafnaaya jaata hai) — burning is NOT permitted

17.3 Key Death Rituals

  • Samra Pratha (समरा प्रथा): For soul peace, a gold or silver coin mixed with curd is placed in the dying person's mouth
  • Nahawal (नहावल): Mandatory playing of drums during the last rites (Dhol bajana anivarya)
  • Post-rites celebration: The house owner serves Mand (Mahua liquor), known in this context as Uthiyar-Suthiyar liquor (उठियार-सुठियार शराब)

18. FOUNDATIONAL BELIEFS & RELIGION

18.1 Core Belief System

  • Polytheism & Nature Worship: They are polytheists and nature worshippers
  • Adi Purush Nanga Baiga (आदि पुरुष नंगा बैगा): An ancient figure who lived in Jambu Island, worshipped deeply
  • Hindu Integration: They also worship major Hindu deities

18.2 Hindu Deities Worshipped

  • Brahma, Vishnu, Mahadev, Parvati, Ram, Krishna, Hanuman, Shankar, Ganesh
  • Lakshmi: Goddess of Wealth
  • Durga: Worshipped during Navratri

18.3 The Sacred Saj (Sal) Tree

  • The Saj (Sal) tree is the most favorite and sacred tree of the Baiga
  • It is the residence of Budha Dev (their principal deity)
  • Ultimate Oath: If Saj leaves are placed in their palms, a Baiga will never tell a lie

19. MAP OF DEITIES

19.1 Protector Deities(imp)***

Deity Domain Residence Ritual
Thakur Dev (ठाकुर देव) Village & Land Protector Sarai or Mahua tree Worshipped every 3 years; white goat sacrificed
Budha Dev (बूढ़ा देव) Protector of Generations Saj (Sal) Tree Goat sacrifice
Narayan Dev (नारायण देव) Family Deity (installed at doors) Doorway Worshipped every 3, 7, and 12 years; pig sacrificed

19.2 Nature & Health Deities

  • Bageshwar Dev (बाघेश्वर देव): God of agriculture (Bevaradh); worshipped while cutting Kutki
  • Nag Dev (नाग देव): Known as Baiga Nageshwar
  • Khair Mai / Maharin Devi (खैर माई / महारिन देवी): Village goddess; protects from diseases
  • Banjarin Mai (बंजारिन माई): Goddess of the forest (Vanadevi); protects from wild animals
  • Dulha Dev (दूल्हा देव): Protector from diseases
  • Ratri Goddess (रात्रि देवी): Protects the Baigas at night

20. SHAMANS & SPIRITUAL LEADERS

Shaman (Shaman): A person performing necessary religious activities.

20.1 Types of Shamans

  • The 'Veer' (Shahdol / वीर): A common man acting as a Shaman. Specialty: Performs mantras to subdue gods during Diwali and Dussehra
  • Guniya or Dewar (गुनिया या देवार): Specialists in medical and magical healing (Chikitsa aur Jadui upchaar ke visheshagya)

21. DISEASE, WITCHCRAFT & MAGIC

21.1 Core Belief on Disease

  • The cause of all diseases is the anger of a God or Goddess
  • Treatment: Regular sacrifices are organized to please the gods and avoid disease

21.2 Disease & Sacrifice Matrix

Deity / Cause Symptom / Disease Sacrifice Required
Maharani Devi (महारानी देवी) Dizziness (Chakkar aana) Goat sacrifice
Budha Dev (बूढ़ा देव) General protection Goat sacrifice
Mhara Dev (म्हारा देव) Vomiting (Ulti) Pig sacrifice
Deshavari Devi (देशावरी देवी) Vomiting (Ulti) Cock sacrifice

21.3 Magic Methods (Jaadu ke Tarike)

  • Supa-Tuma (सूपा-तूमा): The most important method of magic among the Baiga tribe
  • Chirbari Grass (चिरबारी घास): Used by the Guniya to treat patients
  • Mooth (मूठ): Baiga term for destructive magic, also called 'Mar' (मार)
  • Guggu (गुग्गू): A special magical mantra; 'Guggu' is also another name for the owl bird

22. FESTIVALS (TYOHAR)imp***

22.1 Festival Timeline

  • Bidri (बिदरी) — Pre-sowing Phase: Festival held before sowing seeds; worship for a good harvest
  • Nava Fasal (नवा फसल) — Harvest Phase: Harvest festival; new crops are first offered to ancestors
  • Chherta (छेरता) — Children's Festival: Children pretend to be saints and ask for Chherta (donations)
  • Jawara Devi (जवारा देवी): A dedicated religious festival

22.2 Spotlight: Rasnava — The Honey Festival

  • Also known as: Rasnava / Ratauna (रतौना)
  • Main Location: Mandla District (मंडला जिला)
  • Dedicated to: Adi Purush Nanga Baiga (आदि पुरुष नंगा बैगा)
  • Special feature: Worship of bees on the 9th day of the festival
  • Celebrated widely as the 'Honey Festival' (Shahad Mahotsav)

23. DANCE TRADITIONS

23.1 Karma Dance (कर्मा नृत्य)

  • Main traditional dance of the Baiga
  • Performed from Vijayadashami to the Rainy Season (Varsha Ritu)
  • Performed on the beat of the Maandar drum
  • Types of Karma dance depend on the movement of the feet:
    • Karma Khap (कर्मा खाप)
    • Karma Lahki (कर्मा लहकी)
    • Karma Jhulni (कर्मा झुलनी)
    • Karma Khari (कर्मा खरी)
  • Baigas also sing the Laadu song during Karma

23.2 Social & Seasonal Dances

Dance Name Description
Jharpat (झरपट) Part of Karma; man and woman face each other in different directions
Reena (रीना) Mainly a ladies' dance; face to face in a circle (to beats of Mandar & Timki)
Saila (सैला) Performed in moonlight to please Adidev
Bilma Performed at the time of departure of the marriage Baarat
Pardhoni Performed by women to welcome the wedding Baarat

24. ECONOMIC FOUNDATIONS: BEWAR AGRICULTURE

24.1 Philosophy

  • Baigas consider the Earth as their mother (Dharti Mata)
  • Because of this belief, they do NOT plow (hal nhi chalate dharti par) the land to grow crops

24.2 Practice: Bewar Cultivation (Shifting Cultivation)

  • Practice shifting cultivation called 'Bewar' or 'Pondu'
  • 'Kandabadi' (कंडाबाड़ी) is also a similar practice to Bewar
  • Land is cleared by burning; crops are grown in the ash-enriched soil

24.3 Poda Pratha (पोड़ा प्रथा)

  • A cooperative farming arrangement
  • Baigas take bulls from other people and in return give them half of the produce (Upaj ka aadha hissa)

25. CRAFT ARTS & DAILY LIFE

The primary craft center is the Baigachak area of Mandla.

Raw Material Product Made

Metal / Wood (Dhatu / Lakdi) Tarki — jewel worn in the ears (Kaan ka aabhooshan)
Mahlon leaves (माहुल के पत्ते) Dona — leaf bowls for eating and serving

27. LANGUAGE & ORAL TRADITIONS

Reference: Russell & Hiralal's book 'Tribes and Castes'.

27.1 The Baiga Dialect

  • The Baiga dialect is a combination of two main influences:
  • Distorted / modified Chhattisgarhi
  • Gondi words

27.2 Oral Literature

  • Kissa (किस्सा): Stories in the Baiga community (Baigani Koli) are called 'Kissa'
  • Chakwa-Chakvi (चकवा-चकवी): A famous story obtained from the unpublished collection of Shri Sheikh Gulab

28. DISTINCTIVE CULTURAL IDENTITY

  • Noh-Dora (नोह-डोरा): Wall painting made with clay while building a new house (Naya ghar banate samay mitti se deewaaron par chitrakari)
  • Tattoos / Godna (गोदना): The Baiga are known as the most tattoo-loving tribe in the world
  • Pig Worship (Suar Palan): Pig worshippers; a pig sacrifice ceremony is organized once every 12 years
  • Kukda Pat (कुकड़ा पाट): A game played together by both boys and girls

Term Definition
Mandi (मंडी)Panchayat Meeting / assembly of Panchas
Muqaddam (मुकद्दम)Village Head (hereditary)
Tola / Purva (टोला / पुरवा)Circular Village Settlement
Mero (मेरो)Protective wooden wall of vertical sticks
Bangla (बंगला)Living and Meeting House
Kapchi (कपची)Women's waist-to-knee saree
Tarakul (तरकुल)Mandatory wedding jewelry for married women
Pej (पेज)Main Kodo-Kutki beverage (afternoon meal)
Biyari (बियारी)Night food
Baasi (बासी)Morning food
Sunamai (सुनामाई)Elder midwife / elder lady assisting at birth
Jhalar (झालर)Father cutting child's hair at birth
Bardina (बरदीना)Paddy kept together at time of birth
Lamsena (लमसेना)Service-based son-in-law (lives and serves at girl's home)
Karas Kaua (करस कौआ)Sacred deity / symbol established during marriage ceremony
Samra Pratha (समरा प्रथा)Placing coin/curd in dying person's mouth for soul peace
Nahawal (नहावल)Mandatory drum-playing during last rites
Uthiyar-Suthiyar (उठियार-सुठियार)Mahua liquor served after death rites
Bewar / PonduShifting / slash-and-burn cultivation
Poda Pratha (पोड़ा प्रथा)Cooperative farming (borrow bulls, give half produce)
Mooth (मूठ)Destructive magic, also called 'Mar'
Guggu (गुग्गू)Special magical mantra; also means owl
Supa-Tuma (सूपा-तूमा)Most important magic method of the Baiga
Bidri (बिदरी)Pre-sowing festival
Rasnava / RataunaHoney Festival in Mandla district
Karma (कर्मा)Main Baiga dance tradition (Vijayadashami to rainy season)
Noh-Dora (नोह-डोरा)Clay wall painting on new houses
Godna (गोदना)Tattoos — most tattoo-loving tribe in the world
Kissa (किस्सा)Stories in Baigani Koli oral tradition
